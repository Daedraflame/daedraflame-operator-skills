# Example: Horror Stream Diagnosis

## Input

```text
Hormozi Operator: I stream horror games. My reactions are strong and the viewers who show up are engaged, but viewer count is low. Diagnose the bottleneck and give me the next measurable actions.
```

## Output

```text
Bottleneck: Your stream is not the acquisition engine. Your reactions are the raw product, but Twitch is not distributing them.

# Economic truth
Engaged viewers prove the live product has signal. They do not prove strangers are seeing it. Twitch rewards people who already have attention more than it creates attention for them.

# What to stop doing
Stop treating stream hours as the growth strategy. More hours of a hidden product mostly creates more hidden inventory.

# Next measurable action
1. Run 3 horror streams this week with one clear premise: chat watches Daedraflame suffer through choices he regrets.
2. Pull 5-10 clips from each stream. The scare, panic, refusal, or regret must appear in the first 1-2 seconds.
3. Post 2 Shorts/TikToks per day for 7 days and pin the best one in Discord and on your Twitch schedule.

# Scoreboard
- Clips pulled per stream
- Clips posted per day
- 3-second hold
- Completion rate
- Follows per 1,000 views
- Twitch clicks
- Average viewers
- Returning chatters
- Follows per stream

# Need next
Send your last 14 days of clips posted, best clip views, average viewers, follows per stream, and Discord joins per stream.
```
